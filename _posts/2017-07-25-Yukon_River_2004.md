## Working title: Absorption and Fluorescence characteristics of organic matter in the Yukon River

Arctic river basin has been suffering a great impact from the climate change.  
Our study focused on the Yukon River, one of the regularly ice-covered Arctic river basins.
Water samples were monthly collected from upstream and downstream Yukon River between July 2004 and September 2005

Absorption and fluorescence of organic matter (including colloidal, dissolved, and low molecular weight) were measured to examine the characteristics and size distribution of organic matter.

[Fluorescence index] Humification index was consistent with discharge;

[fluorescence components] Three fluorescence components were decomposed from Excitation-Emission Matrix (EEMs), including two humic-like components and one protein-like component. The ratio between protein-like component and humic-like components indicates the water chemistry conditions in the Yukon River. Relatively high ratio value in the ice-covered season suggested thriving algae community activity under the ice.

[size distribution] Asymmetric field flow-field fractionation (AF4) were conducted to measure size distribution of the colloids. Both protein-like and humic-like fluorescence has a highest peak at 1-5 kDa.
